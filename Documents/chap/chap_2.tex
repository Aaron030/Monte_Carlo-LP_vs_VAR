\section{Theoretical Background}
In this section, we will estiablish the theoretical background for our Monte Carlo simulations by discussing the relevant literature on Vector Autoregressions (VARs) and Local Projections (LPs). Further, we will discuss the theoretical results on the equivalence of VARs and LPs in terms of population and in sample impulse responses.
\subsection{Introduction to VARS}
Here I will write an introduction to VARs, their estimation and inference, and the corresponding impulse response functions. I will also discuss the potential issues with VAR estimation, such as lag length selection and dynamic misspecification.

\subsection{Introduction to Local Projections}
Here I will introduce the concept of Local Projections (LPs) as an alternative to VARs for estimating impulse responses. I will explain how LPs work, their advantages and disadvantages compared to VARs, and the conditions under which they are equivalent to VARs in terms of population impulse responses.

\subsection{On the Equivalence of VARs and Local Projections}

Here I will discuss the theoretical results that establish the equivalence of VARs and LPs under certain conditions, as shown by Plagborg-Moller \& Wolf (2021) and Fusari et al. (2026). I will also explain how this equivalence is affected by finite sample properties and dynamic misspecification, as discussed by Li et al. (2024), Montiel Olea et al. (2025), and Ludwig (2026).
